# AI-Era Career Decision Matrix

Derived from the structural assumptions of the 2028 GIC labor analysis (gradual 10–15 year transition model).

This matrix is a filtering instrument — not a prediction tool.

---

# 1. Structural Dimensions

Score each role from **0–5** on each dimension.

| Dimension | Core Question | Why It Matters |
|------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Automation Susceptibility** | Can AI perform 80% of this role’s core tasks autonomously within 10–15 years? | Direct exposure to Tier 1 compression. |
| **Liability Ownership** | Does a human legally or reputationally own final decisions? | Authority roles persist; advisory-only roles compress. |
| **Headcount Elasticity** | As AI improves, does the number of seats shrink, stay stable, or grow? | Value per seat ≠ number of seats. |
| **AI Complementarity** | Does AI increase this role’s leverage or replace it? | Augmentation vs substitution. |
| **Infrastructure Coupling** | Is the role tied to physical systems (energy, robotics, hardware, healthcare delivery)? | Physical systems slow displacement. |
| **Coordination Complexity** | Does the role require multi-stakeholder negotiation, trust, politics, or cross-domain synthesis? | Hard to fully automate. |

---

# 2. Scoring Scale (0–5)

### Automation Susceptibility
- **0** = Fully resistant
- **5** = Highly automatable

### Liability Ownership
- **0** = Pure execution
- **5** = Signs the document / bears consequences

### Headcount Elasticity
- **0** = Shrinks sharply
- **3** = Stable
- **5** = Expands materially

### AI Complementarity
- **0** = AI substitutes
- **5** = AI increases leverage

### Infrastructure Coupling
- **0** = Fully digital
- **5** = Embedded in physical systems

### Coordination Complexity
- **0** = Solo task execution
- **5** = Complex human negotiation / trust networks

# 4. Risk Profiles

## High Risk Profile
- Automation ≥ 4  
- Liability ≤ 2  
- Headcount ≤ 2  
- Infrastructure ≤ 1  

Avoid building long-term identity here.

---

## Compression but Survivable
- Automation ≈ 3  
- Liability 2–3  
- Headcount 2–3  
- Complementarity ≥ 3  

Requires upward mobility into authority layer.

---

## Structurally Advantaged
- Liability ≥ 4 **OR** Infrastructure ≥ 4  
- Complementarity ≥ 4  
- Automation ≤ 2  

These roles benefit from AI scaling.

---

# 5. Velocity Stress Test

If transition compresses to 3–5 years instead of 10–15:

- Does this role depend on slow regulation?
- Does it depend on enterprise inertia?
- Does it rely on friction?
- Is it insulated by physical infrastructure constraints?

If not, fragility increases.

---

# 6. Downshift Risk Filter

Even if automation risk is low:

- Would displaced Tier 1 or Tier 2 workers compete for this seat?
- Does oversupply compress wages even if the role persists?

Account for competitive spillover effects.

---

# 7. Strategic Optimization Rule

Do not optimize for total score.

Prioritize:

- Low automation exposure  
- High liability ownership **or** infrastructure coupling  
- Strong AI complementarity  
- Meaningful coordination complexity  

Then validate headcount reality.

---

**Core Insight:**  
Prosperity will favor system builders, liability owners, infrastructure operators, and human-complexity managers — not pure information processors.
