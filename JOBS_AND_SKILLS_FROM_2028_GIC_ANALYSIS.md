# Jobs and Skills Conclusions from the 2028 GIC Analysis

Extracted from the ChatGPT analysis of [The 2028 Global Intelligence Crisis](https://www.citriniresearch.com/p/2028gic) (Citrini Research). Context: scenario is a *thought exercise*, not a prediction; conclusions below assume a **gradual (10–15 year) transition** rather than a 3–5 year shock, so labor and institutions have time to adapt.

---

## 1. Mental model: what stays valuable

- **Shrinks in value:** Pure cognitive processing, “I process information well,” jobs that add value mainly via friction or information asymmetry.
- **Stays or grows in value:** Designing, operating, governing, or humanizing intelligent systems; accountability; trust; liability ownership; coordination; system design.

**Target identity:** *“I design, operate, govern, or humanize intelligent systems.”*

---

## 2. Formal taxonomy: what shrinks, stays, or grows in value

Itemized list. For each item: **name**, **reason** (why it matters or doesn't), **indicator** (Shrinks | Stays | Grows).

### 2.1 Shrinks in value

| Name | Reason | Indicator |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Pure cognitive processing / "I process information well" | AI replicates analysis, lookup, and execution at scale; human premium shifts upstream to problem framing and oversight. | **Shrinks** |
| Jobs that add value mainly via friction or information asymmetry | Agents remove friction; intermediation built on "navigating complexity you find tedious" is disrupted. | **Shrinks** |
| Routine coding only | Agentic tools can produce working software; value shifts to architecture, design, and managing agents. | **Shrinks** |
| Basic accounting | Routine computation, bookkeeping, and compliance processing are highly automatable. | **Shrinks** |
| Standard legal processing | Document review, routine research, and templated legal work are increasingly handled by AI. | **Shrinks** |
| Pure financial modeling | Routine number-crunching and model execution are automated; interpretation and judgment remain human. | **Shrinks** |
| Generalist marketing | Generic content and campaign execution are augmented or replaced by AI; differentiation and strategy matter more. | **Shrinks** |
| Generic administrative pathways | Coordination and clerical work that don't require deep judgment or accountability are exposed. | **Shrinks** |
| Routine research and analysis | Gathering and crunching information is highly automatable; synthesis and taste remain human. | **Shrinks** |
| Intermediation / friction-based value | Subscription inertia, passive renewals, habitual app loyalty, and "friction with a friendly face" collapse when agents optimize. | **Shrinks** |
| Routine execution | Repetitive white-collar tasks are the most exposed; AI handles "tasks that don't sleep." | **Shrinks** |
| Identity built on "I process information well" | Career identity centered on processing speed or volume is structurally undermined. | **Shrinks** |

### 2.2 Stays in value

| Name | Reason | Indicator |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Motivation and fit | Matching people to work that energizes them remains central; AI doesn't change the importance of motivation. | **Stays** |
| Helping others / impact | Human-centric roles (care, education, health, mission-driven) remain durable; AI augments but doesn't replace the need for people who help others. | **Stays** |
| Autonomy and independence | Agentic work can increase ownership (you direct, not execute); some roles continue to offer high self-direction. | **Stays** |
| Security and stability | Legitimate need; match to industries and roles with more predictable paths; pursue stability through resilience, not avoidance. | **Stays** |
| Recognition and visibility | Can come from human-led roles, expertise, or leadership; use for career cluster fit. | **Stays** |
| Work–life balance and wellbeing | Sustainability matters; some AI-augmented roles allow more flexibility. | **Stays** |
| Challenge and achievement | AI era offers new kinds of challenge (e.g. directing agents, problem framing); use for motivation and path fit. | **Stays** |
| Belonging and community | Human collaboration and culture remain central; team-based and community-oriented roles stay relevant. | **Stays** |
| Financial success (as a value) | Earning potential varies by path; use for trade-off discussions and cluster fit; durable skills support long-term earning. | **Stays** |
| Structured problem solving (when applied to coordination) | Planning and structure remain useful for human-led work and for directing AI; value is in design and oversight, not only execution. | **Stays** |
| Logical/analytical reasoning (at high level) | Routine logical execution is automated; high-level reasoning, system design, and oversight of AI output still require this aptitude. | **Stays** |
| Quantitative/numerical (interpretation and judgment) | Routine computation is automated; interpretation, judgment on metrics, and strategic use of insight remain human. | **Stays** |

## 4. Career paths that remain relevant (reachable from college)

### 4.1 AI systems operator / builder

- **Typical degrees:** Computer Science, Software Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Applied Math.
- **Entry:** AI engineer, ML ops engineer, data engineer, infrastructure developer.
- **Later:** Systems architect, AI infrastructure lead, platform engineering director.
- **Why:** Even with more automated coding, infrastructure, orchestration, reliability, and security grow in complexity and demand.

### 4.2 AI governance & risk

- **Typical degrees:** Law + tech, Public policy + AI, CS + ethics, Business + data.
- **Entry:** Compliance analyst (AI systems), model validation associate, risk operations specialist, AI audit assistant.
- **Later:** AI risk strategist, regulatory advisor, policy architect.
- **Why:** More automation → more regulation, audit, and liability frameworks; does not require elite math, but does require structured thinking and risk sense.

### 4.3 Human–AI workflow design (hybrid business–tech)

- **Typical degrees:** Information systems, Industrial engineering, Business + data, product-oriented paths.
- **Entry:** Business analyst, product analyst, process optimization associate, automation deployment specialist.
- **Later:** Transformation lead, AI integration director, enterprise automation strategist.
- **Why:** Every organization must decide where AI acts, where humans approve, and what escalation/fallback looks like; systems thinking + operations, not only coding.

### 4.4 Advanced physical–technical roles

- **Examples:** Robotics maintenance, energy systems, semiconductor manufacturing, data center engineering, advanced manufacturing tech.
- **Why:** As cognition automates, physical infrastructure, energy, and robotics deployment grow; stable, often high-paying, less exposed to pure cognitive displacement.

### 4.5 Healthcare & human-centric (specialized, not generic)

- **Examples:** Biotech engineering, neurotechnology, AI-assisted diagnostics, personalized medicine analytics, mental health + tech.
- **Why:** AI adds complexity and tools but does not remove the need for human care and judgment; these roles compound over 10–15 years.

### 4.6 Trust & relationship-based commerce

- **Examples:** Enterprise sales, strategic partnerships, high-stakes negotiation, capital raising, deal structuring; enterprise SaaS sales, VC operations, adaptive investment banking, B2B partnerships.
- **Why:** High-stakes decisions and relationship capital remain human-dominated; communication and strategic thinking stay central.

## 5. High-value emerging role types (medium–long term)

These become more important as automation scales:

| Role type | Focus |
|-----------|--------|
| **AI governance architect** | Org-level AI use policy, compliance, risk boundaries, human-in-the-loop design. |
| **AI systems liability strategist** | Who is responsible; accountability structures; insurance and risk transfer. |
| **Human–AI workflow designer** | Where AI is autonomous vs. human-in-the-loop; escalation and risk containment. |
| **Algorithmic risk auditor** | Bias, drift, security, compliance of AI systems. |
| **Cognitive infrastructure engineer** | Agent-to-agent transactions, AI protocols, enterprise AI orchestration. |
| **Elite human-signal roles** | Trusted human brands, verified experts, relationship capital in a world of abundant synthetic content. |

## 6. New demand surfaces (where jobs will grow)

- **Consumption:** AI-augmented productivity, human enhancement, premium experiences, identity/trust and authenticity.
- **Infrastructure:** Compute and energy for AI, cybersecurity, robotics deployment, semiconductors, AI governance platforms, data rights.
- **Human services:** AI literacy training, digital identity consulting, algorithmic bias advisory, mental health resilience, executive AI integration advisory.

---

## 7. What to avoid (structurally exposed)

- Building identity around **“I process information well.”**
- **Roles that shrink:** Routine coding only, basic accounting, standard legal processing, pure financial modeling, generalist marketing, generic administrative pathways.
- **Sectors:** Pure intermediation and friction-based models (e.g. passive subscription renewal, simple comparison shopping) without a durable human or governance edge.

---

## 8. One-sentence takeaway for a student

**Prosperity will favor system builders, risk owners, human-complexity managers, and infrastructure operators; it will not favor pure information processors.** Choose a capability stack (above) and anchor it in infrastructure, governance, health, energy, robotics, or enterprise integration—not in roles that are mainly “cognitive mediocrity” that AI can replicate.
